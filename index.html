Understanding rain, floods, and your area

This site introduces ideas around rainfall, flood risk, and how weather data can be explored on a map. When you’re ready, open the interactive globe to try coordinates, hourly rain, and zone-based flood visuals.

Open interactive map Open in new tab

What you’ll find on the map

  • Weather — Current conditions and hourly rain for any latitude/longitude.
  • Rain zones — Grid areas that light up when forecast rain crosses set thresholds (e.g. 0.1 / 0.5 / 1 mm per hour).
  • Flood depth — Optional 0.5 m and 1 m zones for planning scenarios (admin-defined).
  • Time slider — Step through today’s hourly rain to see how colours change over time.

Why it matters

Heavy rain in a short time can stress drainage and raise local flood risk. Visualising forecast rain on a map helps communities and planners talk about the same place and the same numbers—not to replace official warnings, but to build intuition.

For learners

Explore how latitude and longitude pin a spot on Earth, and how “mm of rain” relates to intensity.

For demos

Admins can draw zones and save layouts (with optional cloud login) for workshops or exercises.

Data source

Forecast weather comes from public APIs; always check local authorities for real-time alerts.
