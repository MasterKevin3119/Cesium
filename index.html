Study Area

This simulation is based on Kampung Asahan, which is used as a case study to represent a flood-prone community environment. The area provides a realistic setting for exploring how flooding can affect surrounding buildings, roads, and public spaces.

Flood Extent and Water Levels

To identify areas affected by different flood water levels, Climate Central–style information is used. This helps illustrate how rising water levels can impact land areas under various flood conditions.

3D Environment and Visualisation

An interactive 3D globe of the study area lets you visualise flood scenarios in an immersive way. Water levels and flood impacts are easier to observe and understand, making changes in conditions clearer.

Purpose of the Simulation

This simulation is designed to help users better understand their surroundings during flood events. By exploring different flood scenarios, users can see how conditions change and how decisions influence outcomes.

Supporting Better Decisions

By presenting flood scenarios visually and interactively, the simulation supports users in making more informed decisions before and during flood events.

Mission

The mission page guides users through the simulation's key learning objectives. Each mission focuses on a specific aspect of flood awareness, helping users understand flood conditions, recognize risks, and make safer decisions when navigating flooded environments.

Read The Water

Learn to recognize what 0.5 m and 1.0 m flood water depths look like in real environments.

Understand Water Depth Risks

Compare areas with different water depths and observe how deeper water increases risk to people and movement. Understand when areas become difficult or unsafe to pass through.

Identify Flooded Areas

Explore the simulation to identify locations that become flooded under different conditions. Notice how floodwater spreads and which zones are impacted first.

Find a Safe Escape Route

Using the information from the simulation, identify routes that avoid deep water and flooded zones. Practice choosing safer paths to higher or less affected areas.
